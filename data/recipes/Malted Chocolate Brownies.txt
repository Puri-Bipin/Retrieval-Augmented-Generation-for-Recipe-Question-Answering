# Malted Chocolate Brownies

PREP TIME: 25 mins
COOK TIME: 35 mins
TOTAL TIME: 60 mins
SERVINGS: 12 to 16 servings

## Ingredients

- Nonstick cooking spray
- 3/4 cup (90g) all-purpose flour
- 1/3 cup (32g) natural unsweetened cocoa powder
- 1/3 cup (32g) Ovaltine Chocolate Malt
- 3/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 stick (4 ounces) unsalted butter
- 1/2 cup (109g) packed brown sugar
- 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
- 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips
- 1 (5-ounce) box Whoppers Mallet Mix Balls
- 2 large eggs, at room temperature
- 1 1/2 teaspoons vanilla extract

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 325°F. Line an 18x8-inch metal baking pan with parchment paper, making a sling, and coat it with nonstick cooking spray.

2. Whisk the dry ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, Ovaltine, and salt.

3. Melt the butter, sugars, and chocolate: In a medium saucepan over medium heat, combine the butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the butter and sugars have melted and start to bubble, about 5 minutes. Turn off the heat and stir in the chocolate chips until melted. Set aside until just warm to the touch, about 20 minutes.

4. Meanwhile, prep the candy: Use a chef's knife to roughly chop the Whoppers, leaving most in large, chunky pieces. Alternatively, place the candies in a small rimmed baking sheet or baking pan and roughly crush them with the bottom of a dry measuring cup or sturdy glass. Measure out 1/4 cup of the Whoppers and set aside for topping.

5. Combine the wet and dry ingredients: Vigorously whisk the eggs and vanilla extract into the pot with the chocolate mixture until very smooth, 30 seconds to 1 minute. Using a rubber spatula, stir in the flour mixture until just combined. Fold in the Whoppers.

6. Bake: Scrape the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread into an even layer. Sprinkle the top evenly with the reserved 1/4 cup chopped Whoppers. Bake until a toothpick inserted into the center has only a few crumbs on it, 30 to 33 minutes. (Don't overcook; the brownies will set more as they cool.)

7. Cool and slice: Allow the brownies to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack. Remove using the sling, slice, and serve. Once cool, wrap in foil, then transfer to a zip-top bag. They can stay at room temperature for up to 5 days. Leave them uncut (and slice as you enjoy) to prolong moistness. The brownies can also be refrigerated for up to 2 weeks or frozen for up to 3 months. Slice and wrap each individually in plastic wrap before freezing. Pop a cold or frozen brownie in the microwave for 5 to 10 seconds to warm it.
