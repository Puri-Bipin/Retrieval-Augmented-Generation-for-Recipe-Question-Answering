# Divorce Carrot Cake

PREP TIME: 20 mins  
COOK TIME: 30 mins  
COOLING TIME: 60 mins  
TOTAL TIME: 110 mins  
SERVINGS: 9 to 12 servings  
YIELD: 1 (8x8-inch) cake  

## Ingredients

For the cake:
- Nonstick cooking spray
- 1 1/2 cups (142g) grated peeled carrots (from 2 medium carrots)
- 3/4 cup vegetable oil
- 1/3 cup honey
- 2 large eggs
- 1 (8-ounce) can crushed pineapple, drained
- 1 1/2 cups (180g) all-purpose flour
- 1 1/2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
- 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 3/4 cup (64g) sweetened shredded coconut
- 3/4 cup (117g) raisins
- 1/2 cup (55g) chopped walnuts, plus more for garnish if desired

For the frosting:
- 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
- 4 tablespoons (2 ounces) unsalted butter, softened
- 1 1/2 cups (170g) powdered sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1/8 teaspoon kosher salt

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 350°F:  
   Coat an 8x8-inch square baking pan with cooking spray; set aside.

2. Make the cake batter:  
   In a large bowl, whisk together the carrots, oil, honey, eggs, and drained pineapple. Add the flour, cinnamon, baking soda, and salt and whisk until just combined. Fold in the coconut, raisins, and walnuts until evenly incorporated. Transfer the batter to the prepared baking pan.

3. Bake and cool:  
   Bake until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out with a few moist crumbs attached, 30 to 35 minutes. Remove the cake from the oven and let cool in the pan on a wire rack until room temperature, about 1 hour.

4. Make the frosting:  
   In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment or a large bowl if using a hand mixer, beat the softened cream cheese and butter on medium-high speed until fully combined and smooth, about 1 minute. Add the powdered sugar, vanilla, and salt and beat on low speed for 30 seconds. Increase the mixer speed to medium-high and beat until fully combined and creamy, about 2 minutes.

5. Frost and serve:  
   Spread the frosting over the top of the cooled cake using an offset spatula or the back of a spoon. Garnish with additional chopped walnuts, if desired. Slice and serve.

   The frosted cake can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
